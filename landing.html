The challenge we're solving

Fragmented data

Building data is scattered across sources and organizations. The same work is redone by municipalities, cantons, and agencies—often with public money—without a shared baseline.

Duplicated effort

Large institutions build their own datasets internally, repeatedly, and temporarily. Results remain siloed, undocumented, and eventually obsolete.

Missing transparency

You cannot decarbonize what you cannot measure, coordinate what you cannot compare, or govern what you cannot reproduce.

Inconsistent results

Without standardized methods, comparing results across communes, cantons, and portfolios becomes impossible—hindering effective policy-making.

One foundation. Many applications.

OpenBuildings consolidates Switzerland's most relevant public building datasets into a consistent, reproducible reference layer.

Our principles

Open by default

Methods, sources, and limitations are documented and public. Transparency builds trust.

Institutional quality

Reproducibility, traceability, and long-term maintainability matter more than novelty.

Interoperability

Buildings last centuries. Software does not. We align with established standards so data outlives tools.
